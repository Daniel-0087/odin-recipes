, making them a versatile and filling meal enjoyed across many cultures.

ingredients

  • perboiled rice
  • fresh tomatoes
  • red pepper
  • oninons
  • vegetable or palm oil
  • galic and ginger
  • seasoning cubes
  • curry powder and thyme
  • bay leaves
  • salt
  • chicken or beef
  1. Prep & blend: Rough-chop tomatoes, peppers, half the onion, garlic/ginger and blend to a smooth paste.
  2. Parboil (optional): Rinse rice until water runs clear. Parboil 5-6 minutes, drain — or skip and cook rice directly in sauce if you prefer.
  3. Make the base: Heat oil in a large pot. Fry the remaining sliced onion until soft. Add tomato paste and fry 2 minutes to reduce raw taste.
  4. Cook the blended mix: Pour in the blended tomato-pepper mix. Simmer 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally, until it reduces and the oil separates from the sauce.
  5. Season: Add thyme, curry, bay leaves, seasoning cube, salt & pepper. Taste and adjust.
  6. Add rice + stock: Stir in rice (parboiled or raw) then add hot stock so liquid level is about 1-1.5 cm above rice. Stir once to combine
  7. Simmer covered: Reduce heat to low, cover pot (use foil under lid for tighter seal if you want). Cook 20-30 minutes until rice is done and liquid absorbed. Check after 15 min to avoid burning.
  8. Final steam & fluff: Turn off heat, leave covered 5-10 minutes (steam). Then fluff with a fork. Stir in mixed veggies or pre-cooked protein if using.
  9. Optional smoky finish: For extra “smoky” jollof, briefly place a piece of foil-wrapped hot coal in the pot, pour a little oil on it, close lid for 1-2 minutes, then remove (take care!).
  10. Serve: Plate with fried plantains, salad or grilled chicken.
